How often should you clean your driveway?

In South Florida, most driveways want a wash every 12–18 months — sooner if you're under heavy tree cover, near the coast, or parking something that drips. Pavers that have started letting weeds through the joints are telling you it's time for a clean-and-reseal, not just a rinse.

We're not going to upsell you a seal you don't need. If a pressure clean is all it takes, that's what we'll quote — and if sealing will actually save you money over the next few years, we'll tell you that too.

Driveway cleaning FAQ

Can you get oil and rust stains out?

Most of them, yes. Oil, tire marks, and irrigation rust usually lift with the right pressure and a pre-treatment. Some deep, old stains fade rather than vanish — we'll tell you honestly what to expect before we start, not after.

Concrete or pavers — do you clean both?

Both. Concrete can take higher pressure; pavers we clean more gently and re-sand the joints so we don't wash the sand out. Different surface, different touch.

Should I seal my driveway after cleaning?

If it's pavers, it's worth it — sealing locks in the color and keeps weeds and stains out for 2–3 years, and the freshly cleaned driveway is the right time to do it. Plain concrete usually doesn't need it. More on paver sealing.

How often should I have it done?

Most South Florida driveways want a wash every 12–18 months. Heavy shade, coastal salt air, or oil drips shorten that.

Will pressure washing damage my pavers?

Not the way we do it. The damage comes from blasting the joint sand out and not replacing it — we use the right pressure and re-sand afterward, so the driveway stays solid.
