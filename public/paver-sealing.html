What sealing actually does

It's not a coat of paint. It's a penetrating layer that bonds to the pavers — locking the color in and the bad stuff out.

Locks in the color

Pavers come out of a cleaning looking like new. Without sealing, they fade back to South Florida sun-bleached in 12-18 months. Sealed pavers keep the rich color for years.

Locks the sand in

The sand between pavers is what holds them together. Heavy rain and pressure cleaning wash it out over time. We re-sand, then seal — the sealer hardens the sand into place.

Locks weeds and mold out

The joints between pavers are where weeds start and mold takes hold. Sealed joints don't let either in. Fewer weeds, less mildew, less staining over the next 2-3 years.

When to seal (and how often)

Sealing has to come after cleaning, not before. If you seal dirty pavers you lock the dirt in. That's why we recommend doing both as one project: pressure clean first, let the surface dry completely, then re-sand and seal. The whole cycle takes 1-2 days depending on weather.

In South Florida the seal lasts 2-3 years before it starts to fade — sun, salt air, and the four-month rainy season are hard on pavers. We tell most customers to plan a refresh every 24-36 months. If you can still see the sealed sheen at year three, you can probably push it to year four. If the color's flat by year two, the sun got it sooner.

We use a penetrating, breathable sealer — not the glossy “wet-look” coating that turns slippery and peels. It's matte or satin depending on what you want, and it lets the pavers breathe so moisture doesn't get trapped underneath.

Paver Sealing FAQ

How long does sealing last in South Florida?

Two to three years for most properties. Heavy sun exposure or a lot of car traffic can shorten that — shaded back patios can stretch toward four. We'll tell you straight at the next visit if it's still holding or if it's time.

Can you seal without cleaning first?

No — that's the one rule of sealing. The pavers have to be cleaned and fully dry before the sealer goes on. Otherwise you lock the dirt, algae, and old joint sand in for the next two years. We always quote clean + seal as one project for that reason.

What is the joint sand for?

The sand between pavers is structural — it locks each paver against its neighbors and stops the whole driveway from shifting. Heavy rain and old pressure cleanings wash it out over time. We re-sand the joints, then the sealer hardens the sand into place so it doesn't wash out again.

Will sealing change how the pavers look?

Yes — that's the point. Sealed pavers have a richer, deeper color, like they're slightly wet. You can pick matte (closer to the natural look) or satin (a little sheen). We bring samples and show you before we quote.

What about pool decks, patios, and walkways?

Same service, same cycle. Pool decks and patios are usually shaded a bit more, so they can stretch toward the 3-year end of the cycle. Walkways and entrance pavers we treat the same as driveways — they take the foot traffic.
