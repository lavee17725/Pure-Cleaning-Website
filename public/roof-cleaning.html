Two ways to clean a roof — and we do both

There's no single "right" way. There's the right way for your roof — and 30 years of knowing which is which.

Traditional pressure cleaning

Our craft for 30 years — and most of the roofs we clean. The skill isn't blasting; it's the right amount of pressure paired with a roughly 80/20 water-to-solution mix that kills the algae and bacteria at the root and washes the buildup away. Done with a trained hand, it leaves tile spotless and unharmed.

Soft wash

The newer method we've run for about a decade. Lower pressure, more cleaning solution — faster on some jobs, and the right call for fragile surfaces like asphalt shingle and Spanish S-tile, or spots like gutters and screen enclosures where pressure isn't an option.

Neither is "better" — it depends

Barrel and concrete tile take a traditional clean beautifully; shingle and S-tile we soft wash. One isn't better than the other — the right choice depends on your roof and the job. We make that call before we set up, and we tell you which yours needs.

Those black streaks aren't dirt

The dark streaks running down South Florida roofs are Gloeocapsa magma — a hardy algae that feeds on the limestone in your tile and shingle and thrives in our humidity. A plain rinse just knocks the surface off and it grows right back, often worse. Our cleaning — whether we pressure clean with the 80/20 solution or soft wash — kills it at the root, so it stays gone.

Most roofs here want a cleaning every 1–2 years — sooner under heavy tree cover or near the coast where shade and salt air feed the growth. A lot of HOAs require it on a schedule, and we coordinate with their inspections.

We don't make promises we can't keep. We'll tell you straight whether your roof needs a full cleaning or just a spot treatment, and which method is right for your specific tile or shingle — not whatever's fastest for us.

Roof cleaning FAQ

Do you pressure wash or soft wash my roof?

Both — we choose based on your roof. Most roofs we clean traditionally, with the right pressure and a roughly 80/20 water-to-solution mix that kills the algae and washes the dirt away. Fragile surfaces like asphalt shingle and Spanish S-tile, and spots like gutters, we soft wash. We decide what's safe and right for your roof before we start.

Will cleaning damage my tiles or shingles?

Not the way we do it. Thirty years means we know exactly how much pressure each kind of tile can take — and when a surface calls for soft wash instead. We never blast a shingle or S-tile roof. The goal is a clean roof that lasts, not a fast job that shortens its life.

Is soft washing better than pressure washing?

Neither is better — it depends on the roof. Soft wash is faster on some jobs and the safe choice for shingle, S-tile, and delicate spots. Traditional pressure cleaning, done with the right touch and the right solution, gets most tile roofs spotless — and it's what we've done for 30 years. We match the method to your roof.

What are the black streaks on my roof?

Gloeocapsa magma — a roof algae that thrives in South Florida humidity. It's not dirt, and a plain rinse won't kill it. Our cleaning kills it at the root — whether we pressure clean with the 80/20 solution or soft wash — so it stays gone far longer.

How often should I have my roof cleaned?

Most roofs here want it every 1–2 years. Heavy shade, tree cover, or coastal exposure shortens that. We'll tell you honestly when it's actually due rather than selling you a wash you don't need.
