Soft wash vs. pressure wash — the difference that matters

Most roof damage comes from cleaning it the wrong way. Here's how we do it right.

Low pressure, real cleaning

Soft washing uses a gentle, low-pressure rinse plus a professional cleaning solution that kills algae, mold and mildew at the root — not a high-pressure blast that cracks tiles and strips shingle granules.

It stays clean longer

Because soft washing kills the organism instead of just rinsing the surface, the roof stays clean far longer — typically several times longer than a pressure rinse that leaves the roots behind.

Right method for your roof

Barrel tile, flat tile, Spanish S-tile, asphalt shingle — each calls for a different touch. Shingle and S-tile are always soft-washed. We know which is which before we set up.

Those black streaks aren't dirt

The dark streaks running down South Florida roofs are Gloeocapsa magma — a hardy roof algae that feeds on the limestone filler in shingles and thrives in our humidity. Pressure washing knocks the surface off but leaves the roots, so it grows right back, often worse. Soft washing kills it.

Most roofs here want a soft wash every 1–2 years — sooner under heavy tree cover or near the coast where shade and salt air feed the growth. A lot of HOAs require it on a schedule, and we coordinate with their inspections.

We don't make promises we can't keep. We'll tell you straight whether your roof needs a full soft wash or just a spot treatment, and which method is actually safe for your specific tile or shingle — not whatever's fastest for us.

Roof cleaning FAQ

Is soft washing safe for my roof?

Yes — that's the whole point. Low pressure plus the right cleaning solution lifts algae and mold without cracking tile or stripping shingle granules. It's the method roofing manufacturers and the shingle industry (ARMA) recommend over pressure washing.

Will it damage my tiles or shingles?

No. We never high-pressure a shingle or Spanish S-tile roof. Barrel tile can take more, but we still soft-wash when that's the right call. The goal is a clean roof that lasts — not a fast blast that shortens its life.

What are the black streaks on my roof?

Gloeocapsa magma — a roof algae that thrives in South Florida humidity. It's not dirt, and a plain rinse won't kill it. Soft washing does, which is why it stays gone far longer.

How often should I have my roof cleaned?

Most roofs here want it every 1–2 years. Heavy shade, tree cover, or coastal exposure shortens that. We'll tell you honestly when it's actually due rather than selling you a wash you don't need.

Do you walk on the roof?

We minimize it. Soft washing is done largely from ladders and the ground with the right equipment — safer for you, your roof, and our crew.
