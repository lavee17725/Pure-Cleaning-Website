Soft washing your home's exterior — the gentle way

Your walls and paint don't want a high-pressure blast. Here's how we clean them safely.

Low pressure on the house

Stucco, siding and painted surfaces don't take a high-pressure blast — it drives water behind the finish and chips paint. We soft-wash at low pressure with a cleaning solution that lifts mildew and algae safely.

Walls, soffits, gutters & enclosures

The whole envelope: exterior walls, eaves and soffits, gutter faces, screen and pool enclosures, and the shady north side where mildew loves to take hold.

Mildew & algae, gone at the root

The green and black patches on your walls are living growth, not dirt. Soft washing kills it — so it stays clean far longer than a plain rinse that just knocks the surface off.

How often should you wash your house?

Most South Florida homes want a house wash once a year — twice if you're near the coast, under heavy tree cover, or on a shaded lot where mildew sets in fast. The north and east walls usually go first.

A yearly wash keeps it from ever getting bad enough to stain the paint — and we'll tell you honestly whether the whole house needs it or just the shady side that's actually growing something.

House washing FAQ

Will pressure washing damage my stucco or paint?

Not the way we do it. We soft-wash exteriors at low pressure — a high-pressure blast is what chips paint and forces water behind stucco. The cleaning solution does the work, not the force.

What's the difference between soft washing and pressure washing my house?

Pressure washing relies on force; soft washing relies on a low-pressure rinse plus a cleaning solution. For walls, paint, and stucco, soft washing is the safe choice — it cleans thoroughly without the damage risk.

Can you get the green and black off my walls?

Yes. That's algae and mildew — living growth that thrives in our humidity. Soft washing kills it at the root, so it stays gone far longer than a plain rinse.

Do you clean gutters, soffits, and screen enclosures?

Yes — the whole exterior envelope: walls, eaves, soffits, gutter faces, and screen or pool enclosures.

How often should I wash my house?

Once a year for most South Florida homes; twice if you're near the coast or under heavy shade where mildew comes back faster.
