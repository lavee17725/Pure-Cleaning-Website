Travertine, marble, pavers — cleaned the right way

Soft stone needs a careful hand. Here's how we keep it smooth and bright.

Gentle on stone

Travertine and marble are softer than they look — too much pressure etches and pits them. We clean at the right pressure with the right solution, so the stone stays smooth, not chewed up.

Algae makes decks slippery

That green film around the pool isn't just ugly — it's a slip hazard. We strip it off and treat the surface so it doesn't come back as fast, and clean the joints while we're at it.

Seal it to keep it

Travertine and pavers can be sealed after cleaning to lock in the color and slow the staining — and a freshly cleaned deck is the time to do it. More on sealing →

How often should you clean your pool deck?

Pool decks and screened patios are shaded and damp — perfect for algae — so most want a cleaning about every six months, sometimes more often if they're heavily shaded or under a screen enclosure that traps moisture. The pavers and travertine right at the water's edge usually go first.

A regular clean keeps the surface safe underfoot and helps protect the stone from staining — and we'll tell you honestly whether it needs a full clean or just the shaded edge that's slick.

Patio & pool deck FAQ

Is pressure washing safe for travertine and marble?

At the right pressure, yes. Travertine and marble are soft and can etch under a high-pressure blast, so we clean them more gently with the right solution. Done right, the stone comes out smooth and bright.

Can you get the algae off my pool deck?

Yes — and treat it so it doesn't come back as fast. That green film is a slip hazard as much as an eyesore; we strip it and clean the joints too.

Should I seal my pool deck after cleaning?

Travertine and pavers can be sealed to lock in color and slow staining — and the freshly cleaned deck is the time to do it. We'll tell you if it's worth it for your surface. More on sealing.

Do you clean screened patios and lanais?

Yes — the deck, the patio, and the surrounding surfaces. The shade under a screen enclosure is exactly where algae loves to grow.

How often should I have it done?

About every six months for most decks; more often if it's heavily shaded or under a screen enclosure that holds moisture.
